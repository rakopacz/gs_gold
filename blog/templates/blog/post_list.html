About The Project

As young girls mature into teens, they are very vulnerable to the impacts of social media, culture views, TV, magazines, and peers. I believe we should be fostering their interests, talents, and unique qualities to encourage successful, strong, and independent girls. Research shows that girls as young as 12 years old can experience a decline in positive self-esteem. Elementary and middle school aged girls can become more susceptible to the impact of negative views on a girl’s unique qualities. Therefore, early introduction to positive self-worth is crucial. To prevent a lowered self-esteem and teach girls that they can be accepted for who they are, a 6-week weekly workshop will be hosted to strengthen positive body image in 5th and 6th grade girls.

The mission of the “I am…” afterschool workshop is to encourage the unique differences in each individual girl. They will learn to develop healthy habits while maintaining a positive self-image without molding to modern society’s standards.

Each week the workshop will focus on a specific topic that contributes to an overall positive outlook on a girl’s life.
